### 1. Key features of Wi-Fi 6, 6E, and 7 vs Wi-Fi 5

| Feature        | Wi-Fi 5 (802.11ac) | Wi-Fi 6 (802.11ax) | Wi-Fi 6E        | Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be) |
|----------------|--------------------|--------------------|------------------|--------------------|
| Bands          | 5 GHz              | 2.4/5 GHz          | 2.4/5/6 GHz      | 2.4/5/6 GHz        |
| Max Speed      | ~3.5 Gbps          | ~9.6 Gbps          | ~9.6 Gbps        | ~46 Gbps           |
| Modulation     | 256-QAM            | 1024-QAM           | 1024-QAM         | 4096-QAM           |
| Channel Width  | Up to 160 MHz      | Up to 160 MHz      | Up to 160 MHz    | Up to 320 MHz      |
| Key Feature    | MU-MIMO            | OFDMA, TWT         | Clean spectrum   | MLO, ultra-low latency |

**Summary:**
- Wi-Fi 6 improves **efficiency**
- Wi-Fi 6E adds **new spectrum (6 GHz)**
- Wi-Fi 7 focuses on **extreme speed + low latency**

---

### 2. Role of OFDMA in Wi-Fi 6

- OFDMA divides a channel into smaller sub-channels (Resource Units)

**Benefits:**
- Multiple users transmit simultaneously
- Reduces latency
- Improves spectrum efficiency
- Ideal for dense environments (stadiums, offices)

---

### 3. Benefits of Target Wake Time (TWT)

- Allows devices to schedule wake/sleep cycles

**Advantages:**
- Saves battery (important for IoT devices)
- Reduces contention
- Improves network efficiency
- Minimizes unnecessary transmissions

---

### 4. Significance of 6 GHz band (Wi-Fi 6E)

- New spectrum with wider bandwidth

**Advantages:**
- Less interference
- More channels
- Higher throughput
- Lower latency

**Limitation:**
- Shorter range compared to 2.4 GHz

---

### 5. Wi-Fi 6 vs Wi-Fi 6E

| Feature      | Wi-Fi 6              | Wi-Fi 6E                  |
|--------------|---------------------|----------------------------|
| Band         | 2.4 & 5 GHz         | Adds 6 GHz                 |
| Interference | Moderate            | Very low                   |
| Channels     | Limited             | Many more channels         |
| Range        | Better              | Slightly lower             |

---

### 6. Innovations in Wi-Fi 7 (802.11be)

- 320 MHz channel width
- 4096-QAM modulation
- Multi-Link Operation (MLO)
- Multi-RU allocation
- Extremely low latency
- Higher throughput (~46 Gbps)

---

### 7. Multi-Link Operation (MLO)

- Allows a device to use multiple bands/links simultaneously

**Benefits:**
- Higher throughput
- Reduced latency
- Improved reliability
- Better load balancing

---

### 8. Purpose of 802.11k and 802.11v

- **802.11k (Radio Resource Management):**
  - Provides neighbor reports
  - Helps client choose better AP

- **802.11v (Network Assisted Roaming):**
  - Allows AP to suggest better AP
  - Improves roaming decisions

---

### 9. Fast BSS Transition (802.11r)

- Enables faster roaming between APs

**How it works:**
- Pre-authentication with neighboring APs
- Reduces re-authentication delay

**Benefits:**
- Seamless roaming
- Essential for VoIP and video calls

---

### 10. How 802.11k/v/r work together

- **802.11k** → Provides information about nearby APs  
- **802.11v** → Guides client to better AP  
- **802.11r** → Speeds up authentication during roaming  

**Result:**
- Seamless roaming
- Reduced latency
- Better user experience in enterprise networks
